THRISSUR

About Thrissur


Thrissur is known as cultural capital of Kerala,and the land of poorams.The district is known for its ancient temples,churches and mosques.Thrissur pooram is the most colourful temple festival in kerala It contains the Kerala Sangeetha Nadaka Acadamy,Kerala lalithakala academy and Kerala sahithya academy.



PLACE TO VISIT IN THRISSUR


NEHRU PARK


A childrens park in the heart of the city named after the first prime minister of India Jawaharlal Nehru and is owned by Thrissur municipal coroperation in Thrissur city.

ATHIRAPPALLY WATER FALLS



Athirappally waterfalls is situated in Athirappally panchayath in Chalakkudi taluk of Thrissur district in Kerala,India on the Chalakkudi river,which originates from the upper reaches of the western ghats at the entrance to the Sholayar ranges.It is the largest waterfall in kerala.

VAZHACHAL WATERFALLS



Vazhachal is situated in Athirappally Panchayath of Thrissur district in Kerala on the southwest coast of India.Located on the west-flowing Chalakkudi river near the Vazhachal forest division and at the edge of the Sholayar ranges.It is just 5 km from entrance of Athirappally falls

SREE VADAKKUNNATHAN TEMPLE



Vadakkunnathan temple is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated by lord Shiva. Which is an architectural wonder in wood and stone is one of the oldest temples in the state and stands tall in the heart of thrissur in the middle f a nine acre walled patch of green.
